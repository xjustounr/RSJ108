Xavier Justo's How to Prepare for a Journalistic Interview

An Instruction Challenge for JOUR108

How to prepare yourself for a meaningful interview.

Assembly time: 25 minutes
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

Do you find yourself struggling whenever you are given the task of interviewing someone? Preparation is a key component of being an interviewer, and by following these steps you will be able to improve your interviewing skills in no time!

  1. Necessary Equipment

    Make sure you have the proper tools you need to help the interviewing process go smoothly.

    This could be writing material or a recorder if you are doing an audio-based interview.

    On most occasions, these are both recommended to conduct a successful interview.

  2. Interviewing Etiquette

    It is important to make sure your guest feels comfortable in the given environment. Introductions are a good way to set a base of understanding between the interview and the interviewee

    Be on time, stay courteous and hospitable.

    Always come dressed professionally.

  3. Have a goal for your interview.

    Have a set reason for what you wish to accomplish with this interview.

    The attached video gives a wide variety of tips to help improve on interviewing.

  4. Knowing Your Subject.

    Partake in background research relevant to the goal that you have for the interview

    For example, if the person is interviewed regularly, take the time to watch or read their previous interviews to avoid regurgitating the same questions.

  5. Have a Set of Questions Prepared, But Don't Rely on them Completely.

    There's certain information you know you want to get, but you also want to put your sources at ease.

    Don't bombard your guest with questions, let the information come naturally rather than feeling forced.

    No one wants to be interrogated

  6. Acknowledge uncomfortable situations may present themselves.

    Depending on the type of interview you are conducting, some hard questions may have to be asked.

    Silence is almost a certainty in interviewing scenarios, use this to your advantage

    Often when discussing sensitive or more personal topics, the best moments will come if you let a question overstay its welcome for just a moment.
